# Privacy Policy — Weatherlane

**Last updated: May 15, 2026**

Weatherlane ("the app") is built and maintained by Kacy James. This policy describes what information the app accesses, how it is used, and what is shared with third parties.

---

## Information the app accesses

### Location
Weatherlane can access your device's location to show weather for where you are right now. This is optional. You can use the app with only manually added cities and never grant location permission. When location access is granted:

- Your coordinates are sent directly to **Apple WeatherKit** to retrieve a forecast. They are not sent to any server operated by Weatherlane.
- Your location is never logged, stored remotely, or shared with any advertising service.

### Saved cities
City names you add to your list are stored locally on your device using iOS system storage. They are not uploaded anywhere.

### Temperature preference
Your chosen temperature unit (°F or °C) is stored locally on your device. It is never transmitted.

---

## Third-party services

### Apple WeatherKit
Weather data is provided by Apple's WeatherKit service. Your location coordinates are transmitted to Apple in order to fetch forecasts. Apple's privacy policy applies: [https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy](https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy)

### Unsplash
Background photos are fetched from the Unsplash API using a city name as the search query. No personally identifiable information is included in these requests. Unsplash's privacy policy applies: [https://unsplash.com/privacy](https://unsplash.com/privacy)

### Firebase Analytics and Firebase Crashlytics
Weatherlane uses Firebase Analytics and Firebase Crashlytics, both provided by Google, to understand how the app is used in aggregate and to receive automatic crash reports when something goes wrong.

- **Firebase Analytics** records anonymous, aggregated usage data: which screens are viewed, how long sessions last, app version, device model, iOS version, language, and approximate country-level location (inferred from IP). No name, email, or other personally identifying information is attached, because Weatherlane does not collect any. Each install is identified only by a randomly generated Firebase instance ID that is not linked to you.
- **Firebase Crashlytics** records crash reports if the app crashes. A report contains the stack trace at the point of the crash, the device model and iOS version, and a short trail of recent in-app events leading up to the crash. Crashlytics does not collect personally identifying information.

Both services are subject to Google's privacy policy: [https://policies.google.com/privacy](https://policies.google.com/privacy)

---

## What the app does not do

- Weatherlane does not require an account or collect any personal information such as name, email address, or phone number.
- Weatherlane does not display advertising.
- Weatherlane does not sell or rent any data to third parties.
- Weatherlane does not link analytics or crash data to a real-world identity. Reports remain tied only to a random per-install identifier.

---

## Data retention

App data you create (saved cities, preferences) lives on your device and is removed when you delete the app. Aggregated analytics and crash reports retained by Google are subject to Firebase's data retention settings, which default to 14 months for Analytics and 90 days for Crashlytics.

---

## Children's privacy

Weatherlane does not knowingly collect any information from children under 13. The app contains no features that solicit personal information.
